Ice cream has been the go-to dessert to cool down ever since the Tang Dynasty! It is not unusual for people to begin putting their own spin to this timeless delight.

Matcha Ice Cream

Over the years, Japan’s Matcha has grown a following for its bitter-yet-sweet flavor—making it the perfect fit for an ice cream flavor!

Wasabi Ice Cream

Wasabi, hailing from Japan, with its spicy-tingly aftertaste would become an ice cream flavor! Its clean, fresh flavor tinged with spice is very unique.

Queso Ice Cream

Queso! This flavor made of cheddar cheese has become a staple in Filipino gatherings—equipped with a cheesy and nostalgic flavor.

Ube Ice Cream

The Philippines has made its mark with ice cream enjoyers internationally with their Ube Ice Cream! Made with purple yam, it is the perfect balance between sweet and nutty.

Persian Saffron Ice Cream

One of the most popular ice cream flavors in Iran. Also known as Bastani Sonnati ( Traditional Ice cream) or Bastani Sonnati Zaferani. Made from whole milk, heavy cream (khameh), egg yolks, sugar, rose water, saffron, vanilla, pistachios and salep.

Dried Red Pepper Ice Cream

An unusual mix of sweet and spicy flavors, which originated in the city of Chongqing in China. Soft serve ice cream topped with chili oil makes for a spicy challenge you wouldn’t want to miss!

Rica rica Ice Cream

Originally from Chile, which mixes aromatic herbs called Rica rica which produces a mint-like flavor.

Black Sesame Ice Cream

Black sesame ice cream, inspired by Japanese flavors, is crafted with roasted sesame seeds that bring out a deep, nutty and earthy flavor mixed into its creamy base.

Mochi Ice Cream

Japan’s unique Mochi has had people eager to replicate its texture and convert it to ice cream! With its many unique flavors and chewy texture, Mochi ice cream has also become popular with tourists in the country.

Dondurma

Turkey’s take on ice cream delight features a stretchy, chewy, and creamy experience. It is often eaten by a fork—not necessarily what you think of when you think “ice cream”!

Kulfi

India’s Kulfi is not like your regular ice cream—it is fudgy, and served in the shape of a cone. It comes in many different flavors too!

Gelato

Gelato, a creamy delicacy from Italy, is made with more milk than cream, giving it a smooth and rich texture that melts gently on the tongue.

Stir-fried Ice Cream

China’s unusual way of making ice cream has made it onto the feeds of many people—often featuring toppings such as berries, then put on top of thick liquid, which will then be rolled onto a freezing pan.

Chongos

This Mexican delicacy imbued with the world’s best cinnamon has captured the hearts of many people globally. It is made with chewy chunks, sugar, and cinnamon.
